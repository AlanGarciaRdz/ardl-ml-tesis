Classical VAR Approach Overview

Estimation Method: Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) / Maximum Likelihood Estimation

Key Characteristics:

  • Point Estimates: Parameters estimated without uncertainty quantification
  • Frequentist Framework: No prior information incorporated into the model
  • Deterministic Forecasts: Single-point predictions with confidence intervals based on asymptotic theory
  • Performance Metrics: RMSE, MAE, MAPE for forecast accuracy
  • Advantages: Computationally efficient, well-established theory, interpretable
  • Limitations: May overfit with limited data, no regularization, parameter uncertainty not fully captured

Bayesian VAR Approach Overview

Estimation Method: Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) / Gibbs Sampling with Prior Distributions

Key Characteristics:

  • Posterior Distributions: Full uncertainty quantification for all parameters
  • Prior Information: Minnesota priors for regularization and stability (shrinkage towards random walk)
  • Probabilistic Forecasts: Complete predictive distributions with credible intervals
  • Performance Metrics: CRPS (Continuous Ranked Probability Score), Log Score, coverage rates
  • Advantages: Better with small samples, natural regularization, full uncertainty quantification, flexible modeling
  • Considerations: Computationally intensive, requires convergence diagnostics, prior specification needed
